Upload a simple categorized financials CSV. Get back a one-page diagnostic with sector benchmarks, plain-English findings, and recommended next steps. No accounts, no data retention, no black-box scoring.

How it works

  1. Categorize last fiscal year's revenue, expenses, and balance-sheet items in a CSV.
  2. Upload here — the file is processed entirely in your browser. Nothing leaves your computer.
  3. Receive a one-page diagnostic across six standard nonprofit financial-health ratios with sector benchmarks.
  4. Print, share with your board, or use as a starting point with a CPA.

Methodology

NonprofitLens computes six widely used nonprofit financial health ratios from a categorized financial intake. Each ratio is benchmarked against published sector standards and assigned a status: healthy, watch, or concern.

All computation runs locally in your browser using deterministic arithmetic. No machine-learning model, no external API, no data transmission. Every flag corresponds to a documented rule visible in the source code.

Benchmark sources:

  • Charity Navigator Encompass Rating financial methodology
  • BBB Wise Giving Alliance Standards for Charity Accountability
  • Nonprofit Finance Fund operating reserve guidance
  • National Council of Nonprofits financial-health indicators

Ethical alignment

This tool is designed in alignment with the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (NIST AI 100-1, January 2023). Although the system uses rule-based logic rather than machine learning, the framework's four functions — Govern, Map, Measure, Manage — informed its design.

  • Govern: Open-source, MIT-licensed, all rules and benchmarks visible in source.
  • Map: Intended use, intended users, and out-of-scope cases documented.
  • Measure: Ratios match sector-standard definitions; explanations cite benchmark provenance.
  • Manage: Tool explicitly disclaims sole-decision use; flags ambiguity rather than masking it.

The transparency-first design responds directly to concerns raised in Obermeyer et al. (2019) about opaque algorithmic scoring in consequential domains.
