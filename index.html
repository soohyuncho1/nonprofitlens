Diagnostic + Engagement Scoping

A consulting tool for diagnosing nonprofit financial health and scoping a structured engagement.

Designed for student consulting organizations — including 180 Degrees Consulting Rice — and capacity-building advisors working with small nonprofits. Combines categorized financial inputs and qualitative engagement notes to produce a benchmarked diagnostic and a draft project proposal organized around a Financials / Market / Actions framework.

1Financial intake

2Engagement notes

Methodology

Six standard nonprofit financial-health ratios are computed from a categorized intake and benchmarked against published sector thresholds. All math is deterministic and visible in source.

Engagement notes are scanned for eight standard nonprofit consulting themes — revenue concentration, market positioning, operations, talent, governance, growth strategy, technology, and impact measurement — using weighted keyword matching. Theme weight reflects density of matched terms.

The Financials / Market / Actions framework draws on standard hypothesis-driven consulting practice, including the issue-tree decomposition approach taught in case interview preparation.

Benchmark sources: Charity Navigator Encompass methodology, BBB Wise Giving Alliance Standards for Charity Accountability, Nonprofit Finance Fund operating reserve guidance, National Council of Nonprofits indicators.

Ethical Framework

Designed in alignment with the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (NIST AI 100-1, 2023). Although the system uses rule-based logic rather than machine learning, the framework's four functions — Govern, Map, Measure, Manage — informed its design choices.

The transparency-first architecture responds directly to concerns raised in Obermeyer et al. (2019) about opaque algorithmic scoring in consequential domains. Every flag in this tool corresponds to a documented threshold visible in source.

The tool is explicitly framed as a scoping instrument, not a substitute for professional audit, board review, or client engagement discovery. Outputs should inform conversation, not replace it.
